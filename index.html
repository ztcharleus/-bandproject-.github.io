Get to Know The xx

The xx are an English indie pop band from Wandsworth, London, formed in 2005. The band consists of Romy Madley Croft (guitar, vocals), Oliver Sim (bass, vocals), and Jamie Smith, also known as Jamie xx (beats, MPC, record production). They are known for their distinct and minimalistic sound that blends indie pop, indie electronic, dream pop, and electro-rock with the dual vocalist setup of both Croft and Sim. Their music employs soft, echoed guitar, prominent bass, light electronic beats and ambient soundscape backgrounds.

The band was formed when Croft and Sim met during their time at Elliott School. After posting demos on their Myspace page, they drew the attention of the label "Young Turks". Working with producer Rodaidh McDonald, the band released their debut album, "xx", in August 2009. The album was a commercial and critical success, reaching number three on the UK Albums Chart,ranking first for The Guardian's and second for NME's best of the year lists among others, and won the Mercury Prize in 2010. Their second album "Coexist", was released on 5 September 2012 to positive reviews, reaching number one in the UK and number five on the Billboard 200. After a four-year lapse between releases, including Smith's solo debut in 2015, "In Colour", the band released their third album, I See You, on 13 January 2017, which debuted to critical acclaim and peaked at number one in the UK and number two on the Billboard 200.

The xx are known for their stripped back meloncholy and haunting vocals. The lead singers Oliver and Romy have been friends since the age of three, and know each other so well even their voices are cohesive. But it's not a romantic or eroticised sound: both Oliver and Romy are gay. 'We're really singing past each other,' Romy once said in an interview with Elle magazine. The result of this, combined with the electronic beats produced by Jamie xx, is a beuatiful combination of intamacy and seperation.
